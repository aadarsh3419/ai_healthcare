Title: Fever

Overview:
- Fever is a common condition where the body temperature rises above the normal range, usually due to an infection.
- It is a natural response of the immune system to fight harmful viruses or bacteria.
- Fever itself is not a disease but a symptom of an underlying health issue.
- Most mild fevers are temporary and resolve within a few days with proper care.
- Monitoring body temperature and associated symptoms is important for early detection of serious conditions.

Common Symptoms:
- Increased body temperature above normal levels.
- Feeling hot along with chills or shivering.
- Sweating as the body attempts to cool down.
- Headache or heaviness in the head.
- Muscle aches and body pain.
- Weakness, fatigue, and low energy levels.
- Loss of appetite.
- Dehydration or dry mouth.

Risk Factors:
- Viral infections such as flu or common cold.
- Bacterial infections affecting different parts of the body.
- Exposure to contaminated food or water.
- Weak immune system due to poor health or nutrition.
- Sudden weather changes or seasonal infections.
- Poor hygiene and sanitation.
- Recent vaccinations may cause mild temporary fever.

Basic Home Diagnosis (Preliminary Checks):
- Measure body temperature using a thermometer at regular intervals.
- Check if temperature is above normal (around 38°C or 100.4°F).
- Observe how long the fever lasts; mild fever usually improves within 2 to 3 days.
- Monitor other symptoms such as cough, cold, or body pain.
- Check hydration levels by observing thirst and urination frequency.
- Keep a simple record of temperature readings and symptoms.
- If fever continues or worsens, consult a healthcare professional.

Management and Prevention:
- Take proper rest to support the body's recovery process.
- Drink plenty of fluids such as water, soups, and oral rehydration solutions.
- Eat light and nutritious food.
- Use a lukewarm sponge bath to help reduce body temperature.
- Avoid heavy clothing or overheating.
- Maintain personal hygiene and a clean environment.
- Take medicines only if prescribed by a doctor.
- Avoid close contact with infected individuals.

When to Seek Medical Help:
- Very high fever or rapidly increasing temperature.
- Fever lasting more than 3 days.
- Severe headache or confusion.
- Difficulty breathing or chest discomfort.
- Persistent vomiting or inability to drink fluids.
- Signs of dehydration such as very low urination.
- Fever in infants, elderly individuals, or people with weak immunity.
